Executive SummaryEventSheet AI is an AI-powered Wedding Operations Workbook Generator. Its primary user is the professional event manager (e.g., Omer Sdot) who operates in a B2B2C chain (Venue → Event Manager → Couple). The generated workbook is not a passive tracker; it is a professional Event Operating System inside Excel and the definitive operational source of truth.  The current market is fragmented: enterprise hospitality platforms (Delphi, OPERA) excel at sales pipelines and room blocks, while niche tools handle isolated task lists or seating diagrams. EventSheet AI captures the high-value space between them by transforming raw client inputs into a fully integrated, automated, and mathematically sound operational blueprint. By leaning into an Excel-as-the-Product philosophy combined with an AI Engine layer, the system turns chaotic event variables into a predictable, error-resistant, and scalable operational workflow.  Critical FindingsMajor Themes & Repeated PatternsThe BEO as the Linchpin: Across hotels, convention centers, and weddings, the Banquet Event Order (BEO) or Function Sheet is the single master plan that keeps execution from failing. When information lives in siloed systems, emails, or WhatsApp chats, operational failure is practically guaranteed.  The Volatility of Headcounts: Changes in headcounts and last-minute guest updates are the highest-frequency, highest-severity pain points. A single change in headcount creates a domino effect across F&B guarantees, table counts, kitchen prep, staffing schedules, and final financial billing.  Seating as an Optimization Problem: Seating is not a static layout; it is a highly charged psychological and operational constraint problem. It must balance human relationships (family dynamics, conflicts, VIP tiers) with physical layout limits (table capacities, acoustics, line-of-sight suppression).  Critical Operational Workflows & User NeedsThe "Freeze Point" Handoff: The critical phase transition occurs 72–48 hours before the event. This is when the forecasted headcount transforms into a commercially binding guarantee. The user urgently needs a way to instantly see the cost, seating, and operational impact of changes leading up to this freeze point.  Multi-Department Synchronization: The event manager must translate client desires into specific operational directives for the kitchen, service staff, AV tech, and setup crews.  Hidden OpportunitiesThe Automated Change-Impact Engine: Existing software records changes passively or requires manual entry across separate sheets. A system that automatically links a guest cancellation to an immediate update in table optimization, F&B cost variance, and resource requirements represents a massive leap in value.  Interventional Predictions: Instead of merely displaying data, the AI layer can analyze the historical behavior of undecided guests and prompt specific, high-ROI actions (e.g., identifying exactly which cohorts to follow up with to stabilize procurement metrics).  Contradictions Between Research ReportsB2B Enterprise vs. B2C Realities: Enterprise hospitality literature (Marriott, Delphi, Oracle) assumes rigid corporate processes, rigid cutoff rules, and structured organizational silos. Private wedding planning, however, is emotionally charged, highly customized, and structurally fluid. The workbook must enforce professional enterprise-grade data structures while remaining flexible enough to absorb highly emotional, non-standard wedding requests.  Assumptions Requiring ValidationExcel Mastery of Secondary Users: While the event manager is highly proficient in navigating operational sheets, the couple (the secondary user) may introduce messy or broken data if allowed direct access. The assumption that standard web-forms can completely sanitize human inputs before they reach the workbook must be thoroughly tested.  What Matters Most (Keep) vs. What Should Be Removed (Trash)What Matters Most (The Non-Negotiables)Index-Linked Relational Sheets: Every sheet must be built as a clean database table with strict key matching (Guest_ID, Household_ID, Table_ID). Free-form text fields must be eliminated to ensure data integrity.  Seating Constraint Logic: Hard-coded parameters for family side, relationship tiers, VIP priority tracking, and explicit conflict flags (e.g., zero line-of-sight for divorced parents).  Dynamic Three-Value Budgeting: Real-time tracking of Baseline, Current Forecast, and Committed/Actual Spend dynamically tied to the live guest count.  Change Log Auditing: A dedicated sheet that automatically registers every edit with standard parameters (Who, When, What, Operational Delta) to maintain governance.  What Should Be Removed (The Feature Bloat)RSVP Engines & Digital Invitation Systems: EventSheet AI is not an RSVP tool. We will not build email delivery grids or invitation design templates. We ingest clean data; we do not collect it from the wild.  3D Floorplan Diagramming: Building heavy CAD or interactive 3D spatial mapping tools introduces unnecessary engineering complexity and violates the MVP philosophy. The workbook tracks numeric capacities and zone allocations; humans or secondary systems handle visual layout rendering.  Marketing & Client Acquisition CRM Pipelines: Lead generation tracking, site visit schedules, and contract e-signatures belong in standard sales CRMs. EventSheet AI begins the moment the contract is signed and execution commences. Risks & Missing InformationFormula Fragility: Excel workbooks are highly prone to user corruption if formulas are overwritten manually. Mitigation: Strict structural data locking and sheet protection on all calculation blocks, routing edits only through designated input tables.  Algorithmic Limits of Gemini/Codex: Resolving large-scale, multi-variable optimization problems (like 500+ guest seating arrays with competing constraints) can exceed the immediate code-generation parameters of the LLM in one pass. Mitigation: The AI will not attempt complex, iterative real-time seating calculations inside the sheet; it will structure clear deterministic scoring rules using standard Excel formulas (SUMIFS, COUNTIFS, XLOOKUP) to flag human calculation errors.
